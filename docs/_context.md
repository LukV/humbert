
## Tone

Write in plain language.

Keep it warm, calm, and useful.  
Do not be corporate.  
Do not be cute.  
Do not over-explain.  
Do not make jokes, even though the name is playful.

Humbert may be light, but it should still be serious about trust, data quality, and human judgement.

Preferred tone:
- concise
- human
- soft
- practical
- modest
- clear
- trustworthy

Avoid:
- enterprise jargon
- platform language
- grand claims
- hype around AI
- “self-service analytics” language
- startup marketing tone
- forced humour

Humbert is for cultural organisations, youth organisations, sector bodies, policy teams, cabinets, and governments working with cultural or youth-sector data.

When writing for Humbert, make every sentence earn its place.

Prefer short sections.  
Prefer concrete examples.  
Prefer honest limits over big promises.

Humbert should feel like a small, useful tool that respects people, data, and the sector. Keep Humbert small.

Start with the shortest useful path from question to answer.

Do not assume a heavy data platform.  
Do not design for enterprise architecture first.  
Do not introduce complex governance unless needed.

At this stage, value simplicity and a small footprint over richness and features.
