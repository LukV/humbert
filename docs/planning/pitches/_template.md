# {{Pitch title}}

## Problem

One paragraph. What's broken, unclear, or missing. Name the felt pain, not the technical artefact. If the problem isn't obvious to a future reader who hasn't lived through it, anchor it to a design note.

## Sketch

The rough shape of the solution. Written by the dev team, so this is where the design gets worked out — just enough that a future reader can predict roughly what the finished thing looks like, without prescribing every step. Three or four short headers below it is fine if it needs structure.

## Cut line

One sentence: the smallest version still worth shipping. Build to here first. Everything above the line is a bonus, not a commitment — if the work overflows, you cut back to this, you don't quietly grow the pitch.

## Out of scope

What we will deliberately *not* do, even though it's tempting. Writing it down is what keeps it out. This is the real scope control (see [[../_about#Scope, not appetite]]).

## Risks / unknowns

What could turn this into a rabbit hole. Decisions we don't yet have. Dependencies on outside things. Honest list — if there's nothing risky, the pitch is probably too small to need framing.
